# Alberta Liquor Regulatory Policy (AGLC)

**HBR Version:** 1.2
**Last verified:** 2026-05-16
**Governing act:** Gaming, Liquor and Cannabis Act (Alberta)
**Regulatory body:** Alberta Gaming, Liquor and Cannabis Commission (AGLC)
**Key handbooks:** Liquor Agency Handbook (Dec 11, 2025), Liquor Licensee Handbook (Mar 26, 2026), Retail Liquor Store Handbook
**Authority:** Board policies signed by Larry Spagnolo (LA Handbook), Len Rhodes (LH/RLSH)

## Overview

The AGLC oversees all aspects of Alberta's liquor industry including manufacturing, sale, distribution, and consumption. Alberta operates a privatized retail model — AGLC does not operate retail stores but regulates all licensees and controls the wholesale distribution system through Connect Logistics Services Ltd. (CLS). AGLC is the legal importer of record for all liquor products in Alberta. In 1993, Alberta became the first Canadian province to privatize liquor retailing, creating the open and competitive market that exists today with over 1,600 liquor stores.

**Default-deny principle (s.1.1.8):** An activity not specifically permitted under the AGLC handbook guidelines is prohibited. For L2LAAF, this means any marketplace feature involving liquor that is not explicitly authorized by AGLC policy must be assumed prohibited until confirmed otherwise.

## Definitions

AGLC defines "liquor" as beverages intended for human consumption containing over 1% alcohol by volume, including spirits, wine, liqueur, coolers, cider, and beer (s.1.2.1j). "Licensee" means an individual, partnership, or corporation holding a Class A–D liquor licence (s.1.2.1i). "Liquor agency" means a corporation or individual representing a liquor supplier in the sale of the supplier's liquor (s.1.2.1k).

## AGLC as Importer of Record

All liquor products in Alberta must be sold through AGLC (s.3.1.1). AGLC is the purchaser (importer of record) while the liquor agency acts as the consignee. Manufacturers or their agents ship products to the privately operated central AGLC warehouse (CLS in St. Albert). AGLC pays the designated payee (manufacturer or agency) for their products after the product is sold to a licensee — payments are made weekly for all product sold the previous week (s.3.7.1). AGLC also collects the provincial liquor markup as well as any federal liquor taxes and levies. All shipping to the AGLC warehouse is on a consignment basis (s.3.5.2). Products held at the warehouse cannot be shipped out of Alberta (s.3.17.1d). A liquor agency is prohibited from selling liquor other than through AGLC (s.2.1.7).

## Agency Registration

A supplier must either appoint a registered agency or register its own company as a liquor agency to represent its products (s.2.1.3). When a supplier engages a registered agency, the supplier must complete a Letter of Authorization (s.3.1.2) and the agency must complete a Letter of Understanding (s.3.1.3). An agency registration has a two-year term (s.2.1.5). The registration may be terminated if the agency has not shipped a liquor product to the Alberta marketplace for six months. A supplier licensed as a Class E manufacturer in Alberta is automatically registered as a liquor agency (s.2.1.2). An agency may also employ a sub-agent (another registered agency) to represent its products (s.2.1.4).

Registration is not transferable (s.2.4.1). A sale, assignment, or transfer of 5% or more of a liquor business must be reported to and approved by AGLC prior to the effective date (s.2.4.2). Agencies may not accept deposits for liquor aside from futures (s.2.1.8).

## Competitive and Prohibitive Relationships

The relationship between a supplier/agency and a licensee must be competitive in nature (s.2.5.1). A licensee or their associates/employees are not permitted to own, operate, or manage a supplier or agency (s.2.5.2), act as an advisor or provide financial support to a supplier/agency (s.2.5.4), or be employed by a supplier/agency without AGLC approval (s.2.5.3). A supplier or agency cannot be set up to provide an exclusive product to a licensee or group of licensees through the allocation process or any other means (s.2.5.5).

## Product Registration

All liquor products sold in Alberta must be registered with AGLC through the Liquor Agency Portal (LAP) (s.3.1.7). Each registered product receives a unique AGLC product code. Registration takes up to **three weeks** to process; issues identified during review must be resolved before completion and may further delay (s.3.1.7). A product cannot be sold until its registration status in LAP is marked "Completed." Each product must not be represented by more than one agency at the same time (s.3.1.4), and each product must have its own unique registration — one product code cannot be used for multiple products (s.3.1.10).

Before registering a product in LAP, beverage containers must first be registered with the Beverage Container Management Board (BCMB) under the Alberta Recycling Regulation (s.3.12.1-2). All products must meet Canadian labelling requirements (s.3.1.15). Product descriptions must accurately reflect the actual product, including consistency between the registered name and the label name (s.3.1.9). Products with no activity for 12 months are automatically archived by AGLC (s.3.3.3).

**Private labels (s.3.11):** Products bearing a licensee's name or trademark must be approved by AGLC, priced at or above the lowest general listed product in the same category, and may only be sold by the licensee whose name appears on the label. Cannot be sold between Class D licensees.

## Legal Drinking Age

The legal drinking age in Alberta is **18 years**. No person under 18 may purchase, possess, or consume alcohol. Licensees must request ID from anyone who appears under 25 (s.2.3.7c).

## ProServe Certification and Delivery Staff

ProServe Liquor Staff Training certification is mandatory for the following roles (s.2.3.2a): licensed premises owners who directly manage, managers, supervisors, retailers, bartenders, servers, greeters, hosts, **delivery service drivers**, security staff (directly employed or contracted), and security company owners/managers. It is also mandatory for liquor agency employees whose duties include tastings and sampling (s.2.3.2d).

Key ProServe rules for delivery operations:

- **Certification expires after 5 years** and must be renewed by retaking the program and passing the exam before expiry (s.2.3.5).
- **New hires must be certified within 30 days** of their employment start date (s.2.3.4a).
- **Proof of certification** must be shown on request of an AGLC Inspector: printed certificate with QR code, or a clear digital image saved on the staff member's mobile device (s.2.3.6).
- **Equivalency** may be granted for certificates from other provinces if acquired within the last 5 years (s.2.3.1).
- **Conditional status:** A worker's ProServe is placed into conditional status for: supplying liquor to a minor (GLCA s.75), supplying to an intoxicated person (GLCA s.75.1), or failure to check ID for persons appearing under 25 (s.2.3.7). The worker has 30 days to recertify or the certificate is cancelled (s.2.3.8).
- **Permanent ban:** Three violations within a five-year period makes the worker permanently ineligible for ProServe certification (s.2.3.11).

## Delivery Requirements

Liquor delivery from licensed premises is permitted under the following conditions:

1. **ProServe certification:** All delivery drivers must hold valid ProServe (see above for full details including 30-day new hire grace, conditional status triggers, and permanent ban threshold).
2. **Age verification at delivery:** When delivering to someone who appears under 25, the delivery person must check photograph identification (Licensee Handbook s.3.7.4e).
3. **No delivery to prohibited persons:** Liquor must not be delivered to a minor or intoxicated person (Licensee Handbook s.3.7.4d).
4. **Sealed containers:** All delivered liquor must be in sealed, tamper-proof commercial containers with contents clearly identified on the label.
5. **Delivery within Alberta only:** Licensees must deliver within Alberta (Liquor Delivery Service bulletin, May 2024).
6. **Delivery timing:** A liquor delivery order must leave the licensed premises during regular business hours; however, delivery may continue for 30 minutes after closing (Licensee Handbook s.3.7.5).
7. **Off-sales hours:** Off-premises sales (including delivery orders placed) are permitted from 9:00 AM to 2:50 AM Mountain Time.
8. **Storage:** Delivery service licensees are permitted to store liquor on their licensed premises or another location specified on their licence (amended Dec 2025). They must still purchase from a retail liquor store, general merchandise store, or off-sales licensee.
9. **Common carriers:** Licensees may use a common carrier (a business providing merchandise transportation services to the general public in compliance with commercial transportation laws in Alberta) for liquor delivery.
10. **Delivery service advertising restriction:** Class D liquor delivery service advertising must not promote the use, sale, or consumption of liquor (Licensee Handbook s.3.7.6).

## Class A Licensee Delivery

Class A licensees (restaurants, bars) may sell liquor for off-site consumption via takeout or delivery, with or without a food purchase. Mixed drinks from the in-house menu may be delivered, provided drinks are made only after the customer order is received and packaged in sealed, tamper-proof containers.

## Pricing and Wholesale Structure

The Government of Alberta sets markup rates collected by AGLC. The wholesale price consists of the invoice price (set by manufacturer/agency) plus recycling fee, excise duty (if applicable), provincial markup, GST, and container deposit. **All licensees pay the same wholesale price** for a given product regardless of quantity purchased (s.3.8.1). Retailers then set their own shelf/delivery price. Agencies **are not permitted to impose a minimum order quantity** per product per licensee (s.3.6.5), but may impose a maximum (s.3.6.7).

Product price changes are permitted **once per week**. Deadlines are 4:00 PM Wednesday for implementation on the Friday of the following week (s.3.8.2). If the deadline falls on a statutory holiday, it remains the deadline date. Pricing of "New Listing" products may be changed at any time until the initial release of the first shipment (s.3.8.3). Per-unit pricing must be consistent across case configurations (s.3.8.4).

See `rules.json` for the full markup rate table. An additional ad valorem markup applies to wine products with invoice prices above $15/L. Small manufacturers meeting AWP thresholds qualify for reduced rates.

## Warehousing and Distribution

**Central AGLC Authorized Warehouse (CLS):** Stores and distributes spirits, wine, coolers, and beer (s.1.2.1b.i). **Other Authorized Warehouses:** Distribute domestic beer and specified refreshment beverage products only (s.1.2.1b.ii).

Key warehousing rules relevant to PROOF marketplace:

- All warehousing charges are determined by CLS and automatically deducted from consignment payments (s.3.13.1). If payments do not cover charges, the agency must pay the difference (s.3.13.3).
- Non-Registered ASN Surcharge and Non-Registered Product Listing Surcharge apply when agencies don't follow proper registration and shipping notice procedures (s.3.13.1).
- If outstanding warehouse charges are unpaid within the notification period, product is deemed abandoned and may be destroyed, and the agency is barred from bringing new inventory (s.3.13.4).
- AGLC insures liquor from warehouse acceptance (signed delivery receipt) to outbound shipment (bill of lading). Settlement at agency's invoice price (s.3.10).
- Product with no sales for 24 consecutive months, or where the agency's registration has lapsed >90 days, or where the agency has not responded to AGLC communication within 90 days, is considered abandoned and may be destroyed per GLCA s.114(2) (s.3.14.1).

## Advertising and Promotion Restrictions

All marketing must comply with the GLCA, AGLC handbook policies, and the CRTC Code for Broadcast Advertising of Alcoholic Beverages (s.4.1.2). Advertising must be accurate, verifiable, and within the limits of good taste (s.4.1.3).

**Content prohibitions (s.4.1.4, s.4.1.5, s.4.2.1):**
- Must not encourage non-drinkers to consume liquor
- Must not promote irresponsible consumption or service
- Must not show heavy or prolonged consumption
- Must not imply health benefits from liquor
- Must not disparage another company, business, or product
- Must not appeal to minors or be placed in media targeted at minors
- No minor or anyone who may reasonably be mistaken for a minor may appear in liquor advertising
- No children's fairy tales, jingles, nursery rhymes, songs, musical themes, or fictional characters from children's books
- In drinking scenes: max one drink per person shown in a social setting
- No person shown with liquor before or while operating any vehicle or doing any dangerous activity
- Comparative price advertising is allowed but must not disparage competitors
- Class D delivery service advertising must not promote the use, sale, or consumption of liquor

**Financial restrictions:**
- An agency is not allowed to pay any advertising costs for a licensee, directly or indirectly (s.4.1.9)
- Co-operative advertising is permitted only if the licensee pays all costs and keeps records for 2 years (s.4.1.8)
- Licensees cannot receive or request any benefit from an agency for advertising its brands (s.4.1.10b)

## Prohibited Inducements

Agencies are prohibited from directing any promotional activity or items to a licensee that could directly benefit the licensee or their staff (s.5.2.1). Licensees are prohibited from asking for or receiving such inducements (s.5.2.2). Penalties under GLCR s.81(e) and s.82(2) range from licence cancellation to a fine up to the entire value of the inducement.

**Specifically prohibited (s.5.2.6):** Cash, rebates, coupons, credits; deposits into licensee accounts; free liquor (except for sampling per s.5.6); compensation for interior decorating, renovations, furniture, kitchen/bar equipment, fixtures, refrigeration/dispensing equipment (with limited exceptions), or building infrastructure and utilities.

**Also prohibited:** Agency participation in licensee loyalty programs (s.5.2.3); paying for licensee recreational/leisure activities (s.5.2.5).

**Permitted non-essential items (s.5.2.13):** Agencies may provide: aprons/hats, bar towels, bottle openers, branded interior signs (must not display licensee's business name), clocks, coasters, condiment caddies, draught tap handles, drip mats, flags/banners, fruit slicers, glasses/mugs, ice buckets, inflatables, lapel pins, menu printing, mirrors, napkins, patio umbrellas, place mats, portable patio heaters, posters, promotional fridges and racks (within size limits), sandwich boards, sanitation stations, serving trays, tent cards. These may be branded/co-branded but cannot be sold/provided to customers, and pricing on items must match the licensee's posted prices (s.5.2.14).

## Liquor Tastings

Maximum tasting sizes per serving (s.5.5.2): beer/ciders 112ml (4oz), refreshment beverages 112ml (4oz), wine 56ml (2oz), spirits 28ml (1oz), liqueurs 28ml (1oz). Agencies cannot charge for tastings (s.5.5.3). Tastings must not be conducted outside the licensed premises (s.5.5.5). When a Class D licensee conducts tastings on behalf of an agency, staffing costs are capped at $25/hour (s.5.5.6a.iii).

## Product Recalls and Faulty Product

When AGLC issues a product recall, licensees must immediately suspend sales and remove products from shelves (s.3.23.12). Faulty product claims must be filed within 30 days of delivery; customer returns up to 1 year (s.3.23.4). Destruction of faulty product requires AGLC written consent, performance by a licensee employee with a witness present, and video recording retained for 90 days (s.3.23.8).

## L2LAAF Implementation Notes

**Kaskflow (consumer-facing):**
- The age gate must be enforced at two points: checkout (buyer confirms age) and delivery (driver verifies ID). The under-25 photo ID check must be enforced at delivery.
- ProServe certification status must be verified for any delivery driver before they can be assigned alcohol delivery orders. Track violation count per driver — 3 violations in 5 years triggers permanent ban from alcohol delivery.
- New drivers have a 30-day ProServe grace period from hire date. System must track and enforce the certification deadline.
- Off-sales hours must be enforced in the order placement flow: 9:00 AM – 2:50 AM Mountain Time.
- Delivery timing must enforce the 30-minute grace period after closing.
- All alcohol listings must display only AGLC-registered products (products with a valid AGLC product code).
- The platform must comply with all advertising content restrictions when displaying product listings and promotional content.
- Delivery service advertising on the platform must not promote the use, sale, or consumption of liquor — only the availability of the delivery service itself.

**PROOF (B2B marketplace):**
- All B2B transactions for agency-represented products must flow through the AGLC wholesale system. PROOF cannot facilitate direct agency-to-licensee sales outside AGLC.
- Must not impose minimum order quantities on behalf of agencies (s.3.6.5). May support maximum order quantities.
- Must not facilitate exclusive product arrangements between agencies and specific licensees (s.2.5.5).
- Must not allow agencies to provide inducements to licensees through the platform (sponsored listings, preferred placement, rebates, credits) unless the feature is explicitly vetted against the prohibited inducements list (s.5.2).
- If a loyalty program is implemented, agency/supplier participation must be blocked (s.5.2.3).
- Promotional agreements facilitated through the platform must include records kept for 2 years (s.5.3.2) and must not result in competitor exclusion (s.5.3.4).
- Private label products must only be visible/purchasable by the named licensee (s.3.11.8).
- Product allocation limits (1,000 cases/product code/year) must be enforced unless the product qualifies for an exception or is a private label (s.3.9.2, s.3.11.2).
- Competitive relationship rules must be enforced: licensees who own/operate/manage an agency cannot be both buyer and seller on the platform without AGLC approval (s.2.5.2-3).

**Both platforms:**
- The marketplace must not facilitate any activity not explicitly permitted by AGLC policy (default-deny principle, s.1.1.8).
- Product recall alerts from AGLC must trigger immediate suspension of affected product listings.
- Any 5%+ ownership change in a liquor business operating on the platform must be flagged for AGLC reporting (s.2.4.2).

## Version Management

This HBR uses bitemporal versioning. The on-disk `rules.json` represents the current `ACTIVE` version. The full version history is maintained in Firestore at `artifacts/system_status/public/data/hbr_versions/`.

AGLC typically announces markup rate changes weeks before they take effect (historically on April 1 each year). When the drift agent detects an announced future rate change, it creates a `SCHEDULED` HBR version with `valid_from` set to the regulatory effective date. The current `ACTIVE` version is not modified. The daily activation cron promotes the scheduled version when the date arrives. Orders with fulfillment dates after the scheduled effective date use the future rates for pricing.

**Drift monitoring sources:**

| Source | URL | Method | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Markup rates | `aglc.ca/.../liquor-markup-rate-schedule` | Page scrape | Weekly |
| Policy bulletins | `aglc.ca/.../liquor-bulletins` | Page scrape | Weekly |
| Liquor Agency Handbook PDF | `aglc.ca/sites/aglc.ca/files/*/LA%20Handbook.pdf` | URL polling (see below) | Weekly |
| Liquor Licensee Handbook PDF | `aglc.ca/sites/aglc.ca/files/*/LH%20Handbook.pdf` | URL polling (see below) | Weekly |
| Retail Liquor Store Handbook PDF | `aglc.ca/sites/aglc.ca/files/*/RLSH.pdf` | URL polling (see below) | Weekly |

**Handbook PDF change detection method:** AGLC handbook PDF URLs embed the upload date in the filepath (e.g. `aglc.ca/sites/aglc.ca/files/2026-03/25-12-11%20LA%20Handbook.pdf` — the `2026-03` directory and `25-12-11` filename prefix encode the upload month and the handbook date). The drift monitoring agent can poll the handbook landing page URL (e.g. `aglc.ca/documents/liquor-agency-handbook`) and extract the PDF link. If the link has changed since the last check, the handbook has been updated. Because `robots.txt` blocks automated PDF fetching, a HITL alert must be raised for the L2LAAF superadmin to manually download the updated PDF, review it for policy changes, and provide the updated content to the drift agent for rules comparison. The agent can then propose HBR updates based on the human-provided content.
